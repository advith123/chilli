Home · Varieties

Six varieties we'll quote on today.

The catalogue below covers the chillies that matter most to international buyers — Guntur Sannam, Teja S17, and 341 hybrids. Specs are typical export grades; we also offer mixed varieties on request.

Guntur Sannam S4

Guntur · Andhra Pradesh

The flagship Indian export chilli. Long, slender, deep-red pods with high pungency and good colour — the workhorse behind a vast share of the world's curry powders, instant masalas, and commercial sauces. Stable supply, predictable grade, and the easiest variety to move at FCL volume.

Scoville (SHU)35,000 – 40,000
ASTA Colour32 – 38
Length5 – 8 cm
Moisture≤ 10%
Typical Uses
  • Curry powder bases
  • Commercial sauces
  • Pickles & condiments
  • Retail spice packs

Teja S17

Khammam · Telangana

The fiery export workhorse. Bright red colour with very high capsaicin content, especially preferred by Chinese and Korean sauce makers, instant-noodle seasoning manufacturers, and oleoresin extractors. One of our most-requested varieties for Asia-bound containers.

Scoville (SHU)75,000 – 1,00,000
ASTA Colour68 – 82
Length4 – 6 cm
Moisture≤ 10%
Typical Uses
  • Hot sauces & chilli pastes
  • Capsaicin / oleoresin extraction
  • Instant noodle seasoning
  • Korean & Sichuan cooking

341 Indo-5

Warangal · Telangana

A close relative of 334, but with longer pods and a sharper bite. Trades briskly through Warangal and Khammam yards and is favoured by South-East Asian chilli sauce manufacturers who want Guntur-region terroir at a slightly higher Scoville reading.

Scoville (SHU)40,000 – 55,000
ASTA Colour45 – 60
Length7 – 10 cm
Moisture≤ 10%
Typical Uses
  • South-East Asian chilli sauces
  • Curry blends
  • Pickle & oil infusions
  • Foodservice masalas
